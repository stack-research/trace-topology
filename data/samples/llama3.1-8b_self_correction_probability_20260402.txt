**Step 1: Determine the total number of outcomes when flipping a coin 4 times**

Since each flip has two possible outcomes (heads or tails), and there are four flips in total, the total number of outcomes is:

2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 16

**Step 2: Find the number of ways to get exactly 2 heads in 4 flips**

We can use combinations to calculate this. The formula for combinations is nCr = n! / (r!(n-r)!), where n is the total number of items, and r is the number of items being chosen.

In our case, we want to choose 2 out of 4 flips to be heads:

4C2 = 4! / (2!(4-2)!) = 6

So, there are 6 ways to get exactly 2 heads in 4 flips.

1 outcome

16 - 1 = 15

So, there are 15 outcomes where at least one flip is a head.

We want to find P(exactly 2 heads | at least 1 head). To do this, we'll use Bayes' theorem:

P(A|B) = P(AB) / P(B)

In our case, A is the event of getting exactly 2 heads (6 outcomes), and B is the event of at least one head (15 outcomes).

We need to find P(AB), which is the probability of both events occurring. Since we're given that at least one flip was a head, the two events are not mutually exclusive.

P(AB) = Number of outcomes with exactly 2 heads and at least one head
= 6 (from Step 2)

P(exactly 2 heads | at least 1 head) = P(AB) / P(B)
